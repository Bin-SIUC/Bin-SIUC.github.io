About

I am an Assistant Professor at Southern Illinois University Carbondale (since 2023). I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Virginia Tech in 2019 (Advisor: Robert G. Parker) and was a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Utah (2019–2022).

My research integrates group theory, nonlinear vibrations, and machine learning (e.g., physics-informed neural networks) to analyze and control complex mechanical systems. Current interests include symmetry-based modal analysis for multi-stage/periodic structures, wave energy power take-off mechanisms, and symmetry-aware design and topology optimization for additive manufacturing.

Research Interests

  • Group-theoretic methods in mechanics; symmetry & modal analysis
  • Nonlinear vibrations; spatio-temporal symmetry; PT symmetry
  • Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) for system identification
  • Wave energy converters (PTO mechanisms)
  • Additive manufacturing and topology optimization
